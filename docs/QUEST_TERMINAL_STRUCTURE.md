# SOLO LEVELING LIFE OS  
# QUEST TERMINAL STRUCTURE  
# MISSION PRIORITY SYSTEM

---

## SYSTEM PURPOSE

The Quest Terminal is the core decision interface. It guides the user toward the most meaningful actions while maintaining daily discipline and discouraging negative behaviors. The terminal must clearly communicate **priority and importance**. When the user opens the system, they should instantly understand what matters most today.

---

## CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLE

- **Prioritize impact over quantity.**
- Highlight the most valuable actions first.
- Daily system maintenance tasks must remain consistently visible.
- Negative behaviors must be clearly identified to discourage them.

---

## MISSION STRUCTURE

Four distinct system zones:

| Zone | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **TOP PRIORITY MISSIONS** | Most important actions of the day (max 3), highest value |
| **DAILY SYSTEM TASKS** | Habits that maintain the system, reset daily |
| **SUPPORT MISSIONS** | Useful but not essential, support productivity |
| **DANGER ZONE** | Penalty missions, negative behaviors |

---

## TOP PRIORITY MISSIONS

- **Content:** Highest-value actions. Max **3** missions.
- **Examples:** Deep Work Session, Build Project Feature, Study Important Knowledge.
- **Rewards:** +40 to +60 EXP (highest).
- **Visual:** Largest mission cards, **bright system blue** accent, **highlighted borders**.
- **Purpose:** Encourage deep work and meaningful progress.

---

## DAILY SYSTEM TASKS

- **Content:** Habits that maintain the personal system. Reset every day.
- **Examples:** Workout, Reading, Meditation, Planning Session.
- **Rewards:** Moderate (+15 to +20 EXP).
- **Visual:** **Energy teal** color, consistent card style.
- **Purpose:** Maintain core systems of personal growth.

---

## SUPPORT MISSIONS

- **Content:** Useful but not essential. Do not distract from high priority.
- **Examples:** Organize Notes, Reply Emails, Clean Workspace.
- **Rewards:** Lower (+5 to +10 EXP).
- **Visual:** **Muted system blue**.
- **Purpose:** Support productivity without overwhelming.

---

## DANGER ZONE

- **Content:** Penalty missions (negative behaviors).
- **Examples:** Excessive Social Media, Procrastination, Skipping Workout.
- **Penalties:** -10 to -20 EXP.
- **Visual:** **Warning red**, danger icon, high contrast.
- **Purpose:** Increase awareness and discourage destructive behaviors.

---

## MISSION SORTING RULES

Within each section, missions are sorted by **experience value** (higher first).

---

## INTERFACE PRESENTATION

- Four sections as **distinct system modules** with clear headers:
  - **TOP PRIORITY MISSIONS**
  - **DAILY SYSTEM TASKS**
  - **SUPPORT MISSIONS**
  - **DANGER ZONE**
- Importance communicated through **size, color, and spacing**.
- **Top priority missions should dominate the screen visually.**

---

## USER EXPERIENCE GOAL

- User feels **guided** by the system.
- Opening the terminal → immediately see the **three most important tasks**.
- Daily system tasks **remind** to maintain discipline.
- Support missions **available but unobtrusive**.
- Danger zone = **constant reminder** to avoid destructive behaviors.

---

## FINAL SYSTEM PHILOSOPHY

- Quest terminal functions like a **real RPG system**.
- Rewards meaningful effort. Maintains daily discipline. Discourages negative behaviors. Guides the hunter toward becoming stronger every day.
